    Research Work

    BS Final year project


    The project was done under the supervision of Professor Dr. Wajid Aziz (Citations: 605) and Dr. Shahzad Ahmad Qureshi (Citations: 84). The aim was to design and develop a computed tomography machine using infrared rays instead of conventional X-rays that are hazardous for human beings due to their shorter wavelength. Infrared rays are adequate in terms of their transmission ability and tissues are less prone to damage in this way. Additionally, economical factor is also resolved by going through their lab possibility. The infrared rays are transmitted through the phantom. There are possibilities that the rays would diffract, refract, absorb or transmit. The possibilities other than transmission are considered negligible. The infrared sensors respond to the transmitted rays and the sonogram so formed is used to reconstruct the image through which the light transmitted. At the end of this research based project, we were able to produce a prototype of the machine.


    MS Thesis Work


    The thesis work was done under the supervision of Dr. Hammad Afzal (Citations: 346). Lung cancer patients undergo thoracic surgery, which is a highly risky procedure. Medical specialists need strong evidence to decide whether the patient should be suggested for surgery or not. The aim of this research was to improve the performance of prediction of likelihood that whether patient will live after surgery by employing various data mining techniques. In order to predict the probability of living after surgery, a dataset of patients has been prepared and made available at UCI Machine Learning website, named as “Thoracic Surgery Dataset”. This dataset has been used in various studies and various prediction/learning approaches have been applied. However, the major reason behind the low performance of existing approaches, as per our analysis, was the class imbalance problem. We aimed to address this problem by employing pre-processing steps at first stage and then improve classification performance by using various ensemble classifiers. As a preprocessing step, data undergoes feature extraction and sampling process and then a stacked generalization technique is used to combine results of suitable machine learning baseline algorithms. At the end of this research work, we were able to produce results that were not biased towards majority class and our model performed well in terms of True Positive rate and F1 score when compared to the state of the art models.
